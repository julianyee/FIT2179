How the World Enters Malaysia

A visual story of Malaysia's foreign arrivals, showing who comes, when they arrive, and which entry states connect Malaysia to the world.

1. Malaysia's visitor flow was interuptted, then rebuilt

A strong start in 2020 quickly turned into a long pause, before international visitor flow gradually returned from 2022 onwards.

2. Nearby countries remained Malaysia's strongest visitor sources

The ranking highlights how regional neighbours stayed central to Malaysia's visitor flow, even as yearly positions shifted.

3. Major visitor sources grew at different rates

Singapore dominates Malaysia's visitor flow, but growth among other major origin countries varies noticeably.

4. Neighbouring countries tend to have stronger visitor intensity

Population adjustment reduces the dominance of large countries and highlights Malaysia's strongest regional visitor links.

5. Malaysia's visitor mix is strongly shaped by region

Asian countries make up the largest share of Malaysia's foreign arrivals across the observed period.

6. Recent years show stronger month-to-month visitor activity

Stronger colours in 2023 and 2024 show that higher arrival activity became more consistent across the year.

7. Johor and Selangor anchor Malaysia's visitor entry network

Johor's high arrivals suggest a major gateway role, especially for cross-border movement into Malaysia.

8. Visitor composition differs across origin countries

Gender composition varies across major origin countries, adding another layer beyond arrival volume.
